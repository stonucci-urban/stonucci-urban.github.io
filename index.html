Ambient Light Temperature Study and Visual Essay

Developed and executed a self-motivated environmental study analyzing the correlation between ambient light temperature and campus geography. Methodological Innovation: Designed a low-cost data collection framework using digital photography and physical light charts to bypass equipment funding constraints. Geospatial Analysis: Leveraged GeoSetter to process and synchronize metadata, mapping light temperature variances across specific GPS coordinates. Problem Solving: Demonstrated technical adaptability by converting qualitative visual data into a structured environmental dataset.
